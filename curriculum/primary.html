Primary Education

Our Primary section provides comprehensive education for students from Grade 1 to Grade 8. We follow the Kenyan curriculum, preparing students for national examinations while developing their full potential.

Grade Levels

  • Lower Primary: Grade 1 - Grade 3 (Ages 6-9 years)
  • Upper Primary: Grade 4 - Grade 6 (Ages 9-12 years)
  • Junior Secondary: Grade 7 - Grade 8 (Ages 12-14 years)

Subjects Offered

Our curriculum covers all the core subjects as per the Kenyan education system:

Lower Primary (Grades 1-3)

  • Literacy Activities (English)
  • Kiswahili Language Activities
  • Mathematical Activities
  • Environmental Activities
  • Hygiene and Nutrition Activities
  • Religious Education Activities
  • Creative Arts
  • Physical and Health Education

Upper Primary (Grades 4-6)

  • English
  • Kiswahili
  • Mathematics
  • Science and Technology
  • Social Studies
  • Religious Education (CRE/IRE)
  • Creative Arts
  • Physical and Health Education
  • Agriculture
  • Home Science

Junior Secondary (Grades 7-8)

  • English
  • Kiswahili
  • Mathematics
  • Integrated Science
  • Health Education
  • Pre-Technical Studies
  • Social Studies
  • Religious Education
  • Business Studies
  • Agriculture
  • Life Skills Education
  • Sports and Physical Education

Academic Excellence

St. Charles School Thika has a proven track record of academic excellence:

  • Consistently high performance in KCPE examinations
  • Winners of the Thika West Sub-County KCPE award in 2022
  • Students admitted to top national and county secondary schools
  • Regular academic competitions and co-curricular success

Teaching Methodology

Our teachers employ various teaching strategies to ensure effective learning:

  • Interactive and student-centered lessons
  • Practical experiments and demonstrations
  • Group discussions and collaborative learning
  • Use of technology in teaching
  • Regular assessments and feedback
  • Remedial classes for students who need extra support

Assessment

We conduct regular assessments to monitor student progress:

  • Continuous Assessment Tests (CATs)
  • Mid-term examinations
  • End-of-term examinations
  • Mock examinations for Grade 8 students
  • Regular parent-teacher meetings to discuss progress
